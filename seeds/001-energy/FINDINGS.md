# Findings — Seed 001: Energy

> Solving energy poverty via distributed renewables and open hardware

## Executive Summary

Cycle 001 (completed 2026-04-26) established the baseline knowledge landscape
for the mission. Key findings:

- **Scale:** 730 million people lack reliable electricity (IEA 2025). Sub-Saharan
  Africa is the primary target; Southeast Asia has significant island/mountain gaps.
- **Market signal:** PAYGo solar kit sales rose 54% YoY in SSA (GOGLA H1 2025,
  2.35M units). PAYGo overtook cash sales for the first time since 2018 — a positive
  signal for financial infrastructure.
- **Technology:** Solar PV is the clear primary technology. LCOE at $0.043/kWh
  in 2024 (90% reduction from 2010). Tier 2–3 household systems ($80–$600) are
  the mission sweet spot.
- **Cost validation:** $500/household target is achievable. Reference model for
  100-household community microgrid: ~$91/household installed using LibreSolar
  components + 10-year TCO analysis.
- **Open hardware:** LibreSolar ecosystem (MPPT Solar Charger 20A, Battery
  Management System 5s, Mini PWM Solar Charge Controller) is the leading
  open-source hardware candidate. All schematics CC BY-SA 4.0, locally
  manufacturable with off-the-shelf components.
- **Storage:** LFP batteries recommended over lead-acid for tropical climates
  (longer lifespan, thermal stability, 70% DoD vs 50% for VRLA).
