# 03 · Measurement & Transitions

Two hard problems the framework has to solve cleanly, or it collapses back into age-sorting:

1. **How do you decide who is ready for what — without using age as the verdict?**
2. **How does a person move between phases without falling off a cliff?**

---

## Part A — Measurement: capability, not age

### The principle
Where readiness for a task genuinely matters (driving, command, safety-critical work), **measure the capacity the task actually requires — directly, in the individual.** Age may *trigger* a check; age may never *be* the check (Axiom 2).

### The driving test, generalized
We already do this correctly in one place: we don't ban driving by age, we test vision and road skill. A blind 25-year-old is barred; a sharp-eyed 78-year-old drives. The disqualifier is the **failed test**, not the birthday — and many places simply *re-test more often* as risk rises. That is the model for everything:

> **Identify the critical capacities of a role → test for them directly → re-test on a schedule, with age (or other risk factors) as a trigger for frequency, never as a sentence.**

### Properties a fitness check must have
- **Universal & routine** — everyone in a role tests, so no one is singled out. A checkup, not a stigma (Axiom 6).
- **Capacity-specific** — it measures *the thing the task needs* (e.g., for command: memory, executive function, decision-making under load, absence of disqualifying impairment), not a generic "old/young" judgment.
- **Independent** — administered by a neutral body insulated from politics or profit, so it can't be gamed or weaponized.
- **Appealable & re-takeable** — a failed check is a status, not a life sentence; capacities can be re-tested and sometimes recover.
- **Always paired with provision** — failing a check routes you to the alternative (Axiom 3), never to abandonment.

### The bright line
You may set fitness standards for **holding power or operating danger** (a role/task). You may **never** set them for **being counted as a person** (citizenship, voice, dignity). Roles are gated by capability; personhood never is.

## Part B — Transitions: graduated, not snapped

A phase boundary is not a wall you hit at midnight on a birthday. The arc is continuous; the transitions should be too. The model is, again, **graduated** — like Graduated Driver Licensing, but applied to every major handoff.

### Transition 1 — Formation → Contribution (entering adulthood)
- **Graduated entry** to high-stakes rights and roles, phased with demonstrated readiness rather than a single cliff-edge birthday.
- Rites and recognition that mark the passage (humans need markers; cultures have always provided them).
- A real on-ramp: first job, apprenticeship, mentorship from Phase III, and tolerance for early mistakes (the judgment brain is still finishing).

### Transition 2 — Contribution → Transmission (entering elderhood)
- **Graduated step-down, not a cliff retirement.** Phased reduction of high-load duties paired with phased *increase* of transmission roles (mentor, advisor, teacher, steward).
- Driving/role fitness handled by Part A checks — supported, dignified, paired with provision.
- A genuine *role to step into*, not a void to fall into. The most damaging thing about current retirement is the loss of purpose; the framework replaces the role rather than removing it.

### Both transitions share a shape
```
   capability/role
       ^
  full |######         graduated down ↘
       |      ######                 .::::::: transmission role rising
  ramp |  ↗ graduated up    ######  .:::::
       |.:                        ######:
       +----------------------------------------> time
        entry                         step-down
```
*You ramp in; you ramp out. Neither edge is a wall. As one kind of contribution falls, another rises to meet it.*

## The anti-weaponization clause

Measurement is powerful and therefore dangerous. The same fitness test that protects the public can be abused to purge inconvenient people. Three locks prevent that:

1. **The veil test** (Axiom 5): no measurement rule is adopted unless its designers would accept it not knowing which side they'd be on.
2. **Capacity-specific only**: tests measure the task's real requirements — never a proxy for identity, group, or age.
3. **Provision always attached**: a person who fails a check is *supported into an alternative*, never stripped and discarded.

If any of these three is missing, the measurement system has become the thing this framework exists to prevent.
