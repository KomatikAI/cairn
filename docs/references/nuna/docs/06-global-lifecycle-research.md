# 06 · Global Lifecycle Research (v0.1)

> **Status:** Initial research pass — 15 parallel Firecrawl searches + 5 authoritative scrapes, June 2025.  
> **Raw data:** `.firecrawl/human-lifecycle-research/` (gitignored)

This document maps the **global** human lifespan architecture — birth through death, across regions — and compares it to Nuna's three-phase model. The goal is to find where societies are structurally misaligned with the arc, and where we are **leaning too hard on broken pieces**.

---

## 1. Nuna review (v0.1)

### What is strong

| Strength | Why it matters globally |
|----------|---------------------------|
| **Two-curve model** (fluid + crystallized) | Matches cross-cultural evidence that faculties peak at different ages; avoids the single "peak adult" fallacy |
| **Eight axioms** | Load-bearing ethical guardrails — especially Axiom 3 (restriction↔provision) and Axiom 7 (archive is read, not deleted) |
| **Assembly not utopia** | Cites real models (PACE, Green House, Japan/Germany LTC, Nordic childcare) — critical for global credibility |
| **Graduated transitions** | Directly addresses the cliff-problem (18, 65) that WHO and OECD literature flag as harmful |
| **Research agenda honesty** | Open question #4 (cross-cultural boundaries) was the right instinct — this doc begins answering it |

### What needs global rework in the framework itself

| Gap | Research finding | Proposed fix |
|-----|------------------|--------------|
| **OECD-centric institution map** | Best models cluster in wealthy democracies; Global South relies on family/intergenerational duty, not formal LTC | Add a **dual-track support map**: institutional (North) + intergenerational/family-solidarity (South), with explicit state obligation to *backstop* family care |
| **Fixed phase spans (~25, ~65)** | Lifespan ranges from ~54 (Central African Republic) to ~85+ (Japan, Switzerland); "adulthood" and "elderhood" are culturally defined | Reframe spans as **population centers with regional bands**, not universal walls |
| **Disability invisible in arc** | ~16% of world population has significant disability; lifecycle support must be capability-first, not phase-first | Add **cross-cutting disability lane** through all three phases |
| **Death/dignity under-specified** | Only 20 countries (~8.5%) have advanced integrated palliative care; most deaths globally lack it | Elevate **end-of-life dignity** from a bullet to a first-class phase need |
| **Loneliness framed as elder-only** | WHO: ~16% of all people experience loneliness; highest rates among adolescents/young adults in many datasets | Move **belonging** from Phase III problem to **whole-arc** infrastructure |
| **Middle phase as engine only** | "Sandwich generation" — middle cohort carrying children *and* parents simultaneously, often without state support | Phase II needs explicit **care-load accounting**, not just "protect the engine" |

### Verdict

Nuna v0.1 is a **sound skeleton** with the right moral architecture. It is not yet a **global** map. The next version must hold two truths at once: (1) the arc is biologically real, and (2) the institutions that support it look radically different by region, income, and family structure.

## 2. The global arc — one picture, many shapes

### The biological spine (holds globally)

Research confirms Nuna's core science across cultures:

- **Life expectancy doubled globally** — from ~32 years (1900) to ~71 years (2021), with gains at *all ages*, not just infant survival ([Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/life-expectancy))
- **Healthspan ≠ lifespan** — significant "healthspan-lifespan gaps" exist across 183 countries; living longer does not mean living well longer
- **Faculties peak at different ages** — no universal "peak human" year; cross-cultural life-stage research shows societies *encode* different boundaries, but underlying cognitive trajectories are similar
- **Grandmother hypothesis / intergenerational function** — especially salient in Africa, South Asia, Latin America where elders are primary caregivers and knowledge transmitters *because formal institutions are absent*

### WHO's life-course frame (closest institutional cousin)

WHO defines a life course as: *good start → optimal development → adult well-being → healthy ageing → dignified death*. This is **phase-equivalent** to Nuna but adds two things Nuna underweights:

1. **Pre-birth / perinatal** as arc origin (not just "birth")
2. **Dignified death** as explicit terminal institution

WHO's 2025 implementation framework stresses **primary health care delivering 90% of essential services** across all ages — a delivery mechanism Nuna should adopt as the cross-cutting backbone.

### Regional arc distortion (the global pattern)

```
                    INSTITUTIONAL SUPPORT INTENSITY
    Formation ████████████████████░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  (high in Nordics, low in SSA)
Contribution ██████████████████████████████░░░░░░░░░░░░  (most investment HERE everywhere)
Transmission ████████░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  (neglected globally; family fills gap in South)
Death dignity ██░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  (collapsed almost everywhere)
```

**The middle-heavy bias is not just American or Western — it is the default global failure mode.** Even where families compensate at the ends, that compensation is **unpaid, unequal, and collapsing** as demographics shift.

## 3. Phase-by-phase global map

### Phase 0 (missing in Nuna) — Conception, birth, perinatal

| Global reality | Data |
|--------------|------|
| Maternal mortality fell 40% (2000–2023) but progress **stalled** since 2016 | 328 → 197 deaths per 100,000 live births globally |
| **Sub-Saharan Africa: 454/100k** vs Australia/NZ: **3/100k** | SSA = 70% of global maternal deaths |
| Lifetime risk of maternal death: **1 in 55** (SSA) vs **1 in 14,000** (Western Europe) | 250× gap |
| ~712 women die daily from pregnancy/childbirth complications | Mostly preventable with skilled birth attendance |

**Broken piece we're leaning on:** Treating survival at entry as "solved" because global averages improved — while **the first day of the arc is still a lottery by geography**.

**Rework needed:** Perinatal guarantee as **Phase 0 gate** — skilled birth attendance, emergency obstetric care, postnatal visit within 48 hours. No society can claim whole-arc support while maternal death gaps remain 250×.

---

### Phase I — Formation (birth → ~25, regional band)

| Region | Support reality | Gap |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Nordic/OECD** | Near-universal ECCE enrollment; graduated licensing; pediatric care baseline | Quality/equity gaps remain; US is OECD outlier on paid leave + childcare |
| **East Asia** | High education investment; variable ECCE access in rural areas | Extreme academic pressure; mental health under-support |
| **Sub-Saharan Africa** | Community/family care dominant; school enrollment rising | ECCE access highly unequal; stunting, infection, conflict disruption |
| **South Asia** | Family + informal care; gender gaps in schooling | Child marriage, malnutrition, incomplete secondary education |

**Global data points:**
- OECD: most countries guarantee ≥14 weeks paid maternity leave; many ≥6 months; **US is the major exception**
- UNESCO global ECCE report: access and quality tracking still weak in low-income countries
- ILO youth data: **33% of world's youth** live in countries **off-track** for SDG 8.6 (NEET reduction)

**Broken pieces we're leaning on:**
1. **School-as-formation** — education enrollment treated as sufficient; ignores nutrition, attachment, play, mental health
2. **Age-18 cliff** — legal adulthood snapped worldwide even where prefrontal maturity (~25) and economic readiness diverge massively
3. **Family-as-default** — in Global South, "family handles Formation" is treated as policy; when family structure fractures (migration, urbanization, HIV, conflict), **nothing catches the fall**

**Rework needed:** Universal ECCE + safe-mistake scaffolding as **infrastructure**, not charity; graduated adulthood entry tied to capability; **state backstop** when family formation fails.

---

### Phase II — Contribution (~25 → ~65, regional band)

| Region | Support reality | Gap |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Europe/Nordics** | Flexicurity, parental leave, healthcare, working-time norms | Sandwich-generation burnout rising; youth NEET in South Europe |
| **US** | High productivity expectation; weak leave/childcare | Engine running hot; Phase I and III starved via policy |
| **Latin America** | Demographic dividend aging; informal labor large | Social insurance gaps; youth underemployment |
| **Africa** | Demographic dividend peak now; youth bulge | **Jobs problem, not laziness problem** — unemployment is structural |
| **MENA** | Critically high youth unemployment persists | Recovery has not reached young jobseekers |

**Global data points:**
- ILO 2024: global youth unemployment at **15-year low (~13%)** — but **job insecurity remains high**
- Gender gap in youth employment: at current pace, parity won't arrive until **22nd century**
- NEET rates jumped during COVID; low-income countries showing **regressive** NEET trends
- "Sandwich generation" — simultaneous child + elder care — falls disproportionately on middle-aged women

**Broken pieces we're leaning on:**
1. **Productivity = worth** — GDP and employment rate as the only measure of Phase II success
2. **The wage-alone bargain** — salary without time, childcare, healthcare, or security
3. **Informal economy invisibility** — billions in Phase II globally are "working" but outside protection systems
4. **Sandwich load as private problem** — caring for Formation + Transmission simultaneously with no institutional relief

**Rework needed:** Phase II defined as **sustainable generativity** — not maximum output. Parental leave, childcare, elder respite care, and working-time limits are **engine maintenance**, not perks. Formalize protections for informal workers.

---

### Phase III — Transmission (~65 → end, regional band)

| Region | Support reality | Gap |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Japan** | Kaigo Hoken (2000) — universal LTC insurance | Rural isolation; workforce shortage |
| **Germany** | Pflegeversicherung (1995) — LTC insurance | Similar workforce pressure |
| **Nordics** | Strong welfare + community models | Loneliness still rising |
| **US** | PACE/Green House exist but are **tiny scale** | Medicaid warehouse model dominant; isolation epidemic |
| **South Asia / Africa** | **Family solidarity primary**; elders care for grandchildren | Public old-age support largely absent; urbanization breaking extended-family model |
| **China** | Rapid aging; 4-2-1 family structure collapsing | "4 grandparents, 2 parents, 1 child" — transmission burden inverted |

**Global data points:**
- LTC insurance models (Japan/Germany) are the **only proven national-scale** Transmission institutions
- WHO: loneliness affects **~16% globally**; **~11.8% of older adults** — but loneliness is not elder-only
- Social isolation linked to **50% increased dementia risk**, 30% increased stroke risk
- Global South aging: demographic dividend flipping to **dependency ratio crisis** in Asia and Latin America by 2050; Africa aging more slowly but urbanizing fast

**Broken pieces we're leaning on:**
1. **Retirement cliff** — role stripped at 65 (or earlier in manual labor economies) with no transmission role offered
2. **Warehouse care** — institutional beds as the default when family "can't cope"
3. **Family duty as policy** — especially in Global South, **intergenerational care without state backstop** is treated as cultural virtue, not policy failure
4. **Driving = mobility** — when driving ends, no provision (violates Nuna Axiom 3 in most countries)
5. **Dementia = elder default** — cognitive decline used to write off entire phase

**Rework needed:** LTC social insurance as global template; PACE/community day models at scale; **mandatory transmission roles** (mentor, council, teacher); mobility guarantee; state support for family caregivers (respite, cash, training).

---

### Phase IV (missing in Nuna) — Death with dignity

| Global reality | Data |
|--------------|------|
| Only **~20 countries (8.5%)** have advanced integrated palliative care | Global Atlas of Palliative Care |
| Majority of deaths in low/middle-income countries without pain relief or hospice access | WHO palliative care gap |
| End-of-life often high-cost, high-suffering, hospital-default | Even wealthy countries |

**Broken piece we're leaning on:** **Hospital-as-death** — medicine's success at extending life without a parallel institution for dignified exit.

**Rework needed:** Palliative care as **universal health coverage essential service** (WHO position); home death with support as default where desired; advance directives and pain relief as rights, not luxuries.

## 4. Cross-cutting global failures

### A. Belonging treated as optional

WHO Commission on Social Connection (2025): loneliness causes an estimated **870,000 deaths/year**; social health is "the missing pillar" alongside physical and mental health.

Nuna lists belonging as cross-cutting but underweights its **Phase I and II** prevalence. This is not an elder problem — it is an **arc infrastructure** problem.

### B. Disability excluded from the arc map

~1.3 billion people experience significant disability. The school-to-work transition for disabled youth is broken globally. Capability measurement (Nuna doc 03) is *exactly right* for disability — but the framework must name disability as a **permanent cross-phase condition**, not an edge case.

### C. Gendered load invisible

- Maternal death risk
- Unpaid care work (Formation + Transmission)
- Youth employment gender gap (won't close for 100+ years at current pace)
- Sandwich generation = disproportionately women

Any whole-arc design that doesn't **account for gendered care load** will recreate the middle-heavy bias in a new form.

### D. Conflict, climate, migration as arc disruptors

Maternal mortality, youth NEET, and elder abandonment all spike in humanitarian crises. The arc map must include **disruption states** — refugee, displaced, conflict-affected — where the phases compress or collapse entirely.

## 5. Where society leans too hard on broken pieces

| Broken piece | What we pretend | What actually happens | Nuna axiom violated |
|--------------|-----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Productivity economy** | Phase II output funds everything | Burnout starves Formation + Transmission; informal workers invisible | Axiom 1 (whole curve) |
| **Nuclear family** | Parents + grandparents handle both ends | Sandwich collapse; migration breaks extended family | Axiom 3 (no provision) |
| **Age thresholds** | 18 and 65 are meaningful boundaries | Cliffs in rights, insurance, role, identity | Axiom 2 (capability not age) |
| **Hospital medicine** | Health = disease treatment | Death undignified; aging pathologized | Axiom 7 (archive read) |
| **Car infrastructure** | Mobility = driving | Elders (and disabled) housebound when keys gone | Axiom 3 (restriction without provision) |
| **Warehouse eldercare** | Beds = care | Isolation, dignity loss, family guilt | Axiom 6 (dignity constant) |
| **School enrollment** | Education = formation | Ignores ECCE, mental health, safe-mistake scaffolding | Axiom 1 |
| **Averages** | Global progress = everyone's progress | 250× maternal death gap; NEET regressing in poorest countries | Axiom 5 (veil of ignorance) |
| **Retirement** | Stop working = reward | Role void; purpose collapse; health decline | Axiom 7 |
| **Family duty (Global South)** | Culture will handle it | Urbanization + aging = family structure breaking with no backup | Axiom 3 |

## 6. Dual architecture model (proposed for Nuna v0.2)

Nuna should map **two support architectures** that coexist globally:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    BIOLOGICAL ARC (universal)                    │
│   Formation → Contribution → Transmission → Dignified Death      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              │
          ┌───────────────────┴───────────────────┐
          ▼                                           ▼
┌──────────────────────┐                 ┌──────────────────────┐
│ INSTITUTIONAL TRACK  │                 │ SOLIDARITY TRACK     │
│ (OECD, wealthy states)│                 │ (Global South, rural)│
│ LTC insurance        │                 │ Extended family      │
│ ECCE systems         │                 │ Grandparent childcare│
│ Social insurance     │                 │ Community mutual aid │
│ PACE/Green House     │                 │ Religious/civic nets │
└──────────────────────┘                 └──────────────────────┘
          │                                           │
          └───────────────────┬───────────────────┘
                              ▼
              ┌───────────────────────────────┐
              │ STATE BACKSTOP (universal duty)│
              │ When either track fails:       │
              │ income floor, healthcare,      │
              │ respite, mobility, belonging   │
              └───────────────────────────────┘
```

**Key principle:** The solidarity track is *real and valuable* — Nuna should not Westernize the map. But solidarity without backstop is **leaning on a breaking branch** as urbanization, migration, and aging accelerate.
