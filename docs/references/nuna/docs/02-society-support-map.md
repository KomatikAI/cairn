# 02 · Society Support Map

If [`01-the-arc.md`](01-the-arc.md) is the map of the person, this is the map of what the society owes the person at each point on it. Every entry obeys the axioms — especially **no restriction without provision** (Axiom 3) and **support the whole curve** (Axiom 1).

Each phase is described as: **what the phase needs → the institution that provides it → a real-world model that already exists.** None of this is utopian; it is assembly of things that work somewhere already.

---

## Phase I — Formation (Absorb)

| Need | Institution | Existing model |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Early development | Universal early-childhood care & education | Nordic universal childcare; Head Start |
| Learning | Well-funded public education | (under-resourced in many places — restore it) |
| Safe mistakes | Graduated entry to high-stakes activity | Graduated Driver Licensing (staged) |
| Health | Universal pediatric healthcare | Most OECD nations |
| Stability | Child income support / family benefits | Child Tax Credit; family allowances |
| Guidance | Mentorship pipelines (Phase III → Phase I) | Intergenerational mentoring programs |

**Design note:** the entire phase is a scaffold for a brain that won't finish its judgment circuitry until ~25. Stakes should rise *with* readiness, not snap on at an arbitrary birthday.

---

## Phase II — Contribution (Act)

| Need | Institution | Existing model |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Opportunity | Access to meaningful work & training | Active labor-market policy (e.g., Denmark "flexicurity") |
| Autonomy & time | Living wage, sane hours, leave | EU working-time norms |
| Raising the next gen | Paid parental leave + childcare | Nearly universal in OECD except the US |
| Health | Universal healthcare | OECD baseline |
| Security against shocks | Unemployment, disability, illness insurance | Social-insurance systems |

**Design note:** this phase carries the load for the other two. Burning it out (overwork, no childcare, no security) starves *all three* phases, because Phase II funds the young and the old. Protecting the engine is protecting the whole arc.

---

## Phase III — Transmission (Archive)

This is the phase societies most neglect. The framework treats it as first-class.

| Need | Institution | Existing model |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Care that isn't a warehouse | Community-based & day care for elders | **PACE** (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly); adult day health centers |
| Dignified residential care | Small-home care models | **Green House Project** (small homes, not institutional wings) |
| Universal long-term care | Dedicated LTC social insurance | **Japan's Kaigo Hoken (2000); Germany's Pflegeversicherung (1995)** |
| Mobility when driving ends | Transit + paratransit + subsidized rides | ADA paratransit; senior ride-voucher programs |
| Role & purpose | Transmission roles: mentor, teacher, council, elder | Intergenerational schools; civic elder councils |
| Connection | Anti-isolation programs | Co-housing; community hubs; befriending services |
| Health | Geriatric & palliative care + end-of-life dignity | Hospice; palliative-care systems |

**Design note:** "daycare for the elderly" is not a metaphor — it exists (PACE, adult day health) and it works, keeping people in their communities with care, meals, social life, and medical support. The phase's deepest need is not just care but **a role** — the archive must be *read*, or the person is shelved (violating Axiom 7).

## The close of the arc — Dignified Death (Release)

The arc's terminal stage is its own design target (see [`01-the-arc.md`](01-the-arc.md)). It is continuous with Transmission but named separately because it is where a society most easily lets its guarantees lapse under the cover of "nothing more can be done." Obeying **dignity-constant** (Axiom 6) and **refuse abandonment, not loss** (Axiom 9), the close of the arc gets first-class institutions, not an afterthought.

| Need | Institution | Existing model |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Comfort when cure is no longer the frame | Palliative care, integrated early | WHO palliative-care framework; specialist palliative teams |
| Care in the final stretch | Hospice (home and inpatient) | Medicare Hospice Benefit; UK hospice movement |
| Preferences known before crisis | Advance-care planning & supported decision-making | POLST/advance-directive systems |
| Presence and meaning | Family presence, cultural & spiritual practice | Hospice chaplaincy; family-leave-for-care |
| The bereaved are not dropped | Caregiver support & bereavement care | Bereavement services; carer's allowances |

**Design note:** the bright line is **non-abandonment by futility** — a decision to stop a burdensome treatment must be paired with active comfort and presence, never with withdrawal. Stopping a treatment is allowed; stopping *care* is not (Axiom 9). This is the same restriction↔provision rule the rest of the map runs on, applied where it is most often broken.

## Cross-cutting guarantees (all phases)

Some supports run the full length of the arc:

- **Healthcare** — continuous from pediatric to geriatric to palliative.
- **Mobility** — the right to move through the world doesn't expire; when one mode (driving) ends, another (transit/rides) is guaranteed (Axiom 3).
- **Income floor** — no phase falls below subsistence.
- **Housing** — stable shelter at every stage.
- **Belonging** — protection from isolation, young to old.

## The restriction↔provision ledger

Whenever the framework restricts something, the paired provision is **mandatory and named**:

| If we restrict… | We must provide… |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Driving (failed fitness check) | Transit, paratransit, subsidized rides |
| A command/safety-critical role (failed fitness check) | Another role, retained income, dignity |
| High-stakes activity for the young (not yet ready) | Education + graduated pathway to it |
| Closing/consolidating a facility (allocative efficiency) | The served population's guarantee by another mode — telehealth, staffed clinic, guaranteed transport, relocation support |

A restriction line with an empty provision column is a **design error**, not a policy. This holds for *geography* too: an asset may be reallocated to higher impact, but the obligation it carried may not be dropped — **consolidate the asset, never the obligation** (Axiom 3, geographic corollary; Axiom 9).

---

## How it's paid for

This framework deliberately does not depend on austerity. The funding logic mirrors the systems above: **dedicated social insurance** for long-term care (Japan/Germany model), **progressive general revenue** for education and health, and **redirected subsidies** (e.g., car-infrastructure → transit) for mobility. The cost of supporting the whole arc is the price of a society that doesn't discard its own people at either end.
