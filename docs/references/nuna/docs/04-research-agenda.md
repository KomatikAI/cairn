# 04 · Research Agenda

This framework is a v0.1 foundation, not a finished theory. These are the honest open problems — the places where the design is currently underspecified, where the science is unsettled, or where a wrong answer would do real harm. Each is a research thread worth pulling.

---

## A. Measurement (the highest-stakes unknowns)

1. **Can "judgment" and "wisdom" be measured reliably and fairly?** Cognitive screening for memory and executive function is mature; measuring *complex judgment* for high-stakes roles is not. Without a valid, bias-resistant instrument, fitness checks for command are aspirational. → Investigate existing cognitive/medical fitness batteries; study where they succeed and fail.
2. **Where do the real capability thresholds sit for each role?** "Fit to drive" and "fit to command" need evidence-based cutoffs, not intuitions. → Map task → required-capacity → measurable-threshold for a handful of concrete roles.
3. **How do you keep measurement from being weaponized?** The anti-weaponization clause (doc 03) is a principle, not yet a mechanism. → Design the independent-body governance, appeals process, and audit trail in detail.

## B. The science of the arc

4. **How much do the phase boundaries shift across cultures and eras?** Lifespan, health-span, and the social meaning of each phase vary. → Cross-cultural review; avoid encoding one society's curve as universal.
5. **How is technology bending the curve?** Longer health-spans, cognitive aids, AI augmentation — these may extend Phase II and reshape Phase III. → Track health-span vs. lifespan divergence and its design implications.
6. **What actually drives healthy vs. pathological aging?** The framework leans on "decline is selective, not global." → Keep current with the dementia-risk vs. normal-aging literature so the ethical hinge stays evidence-based.

## C. Transitions

7. **What makes a transition humane vs. traumatic?** Retirement-as-cliff is known to harm purpose and health. → Study graduated step-down models, phased roles, and the psychology of role loss.
8. **What rites/markers do the transitions need?** Humans need passage markers; modernity stripped many. → Survey what worked historically without romanticizing it.

## D. Society & funding

9. **What does the whole-arc support system cost, and how is it financed sustainably?** → Build the funding model (dedicated LTC insurance + progressive revenue + redirected subsidies) with real numbers.
10. **How do you build the political will?** Whole-curve support competes with short-term budget pressure. → Identify the coalitions and the sequencing (which pillar first, which is most popular).
11. **How do you protect Phase II from burnout?** The engine funds the other two; overload starves everything. → Study working-time, childcare, and security policies that keep the engine healthy.

## E. Ethics & guardrails (never close these)

12. **The chosen-vs-conscripted line under pressure.** Self-sacrifice is noble when chosen; the danger is subtle coercion (social, economic) that makes "choice" a fiction. → Define what makes a sacrifice *genuinely* voluntary.
13. **Preventing the slide from "support the declining" to "manage the unwanted."** The framework's whole moral weight rests here. → Keep a standing red-team against any drift toward discard.

---

## F. Global lifecycle mapping (opened June 2025)

14. **What does the arc look like outside the OECD?** → Initial pass in [`06-global-lifecycle-research.md`](06-global-lifecycle-research.md). Next: healthspan gaps, ECCE heat maps, LTC coverage matrix, palliative care index.
15. **How do institutional vs. solidarity support tracks interact?** Family/intergenerational care carries Formation and Transmission in much of the Global South — but urbanization and aging are breaking it. → Model the dual-track architecture with state backstop.
16. **Where are we leaning on broken pieces?** Productivity economy, nuclear family, age cliffs, hospital-default death, car-mobility, warehouse eldercare, family-duty-without-provision. → See §5 of doc 06; each needs a named replacement institution.
17. **Perinatal and death: the missing arc ends.** WHO treats both as life-course bookends; Nuna v0.1 underweights them. → Add Phase 0 and dignified-death as first-class design targets.
