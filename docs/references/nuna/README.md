# Nuna

> **Nuna** — Inuktitut (ᓄᓇ): *land / earth.* The ground the cycle returns to and rises from — new becomes old, old becomes earth and carbon, earth becomes new.

*A design framework for how a human life should be mapped — and how a society should hold it up, from the first day to the last.*

---

## The thesis

A human life is not a flat line of equal capacity, nor a ranking of who deserves to exist. It is an **arc**: we rise into ability, we carry the load for a season, and we hand what we learned forward. Every point on that arc has a different need and a different gift.

Most societies are built for the middle of the arc — the productive, "adult" years — and treat the beginning and the end as problems to manage. This project rejects that. It maps the **whole** arc and designs the institutions that support a person at **every** phase, so that no one is discarded for being early or late on the curve.

The arc has three movements:

| Phase | Movement | Span (approx.) | Core task |
|-------|----------|----------------|-----------|
| **I — Formation** | *Absorb* | birth → ~25 | learn, grow, become capable |
| **II — Contribution** | *Act* | ~25 → ~65 | build, carry, give to the next generation |
| **III — Transmission** | *Archive* | ~65 → end | mentor, steward, hand the knowledge forward |

The archive is **read, not deleted.** Elders are not shelved — they are the file the next generation boots from.

## What this project is

- A **map** of the human life-course grounded in developmental science.
- A **support design**: the institutions, guarantees, and transitions a society owes each phase.
- A set of **first principles** that keep the design humane (support over discard, capability over age, chosen over conscripted).

## What this project is *not*

- Not a plan to restrict, rank, or remove anyone based on age.
- Not a productivity ideology. Worth is constant across the arc; only capability varies.
- Not finished. This is a living document and a research agenda.

## The order of repair

The layers above set the *what*. This sets the **order** — and the order is itself a principle.

A presidency, like a person, cannot care for others before it can care for itself. *You cannot take care of others until you can take care of yourself first.* So the work is **sequenced, not parallel**:

1. **Repair at home, first and foremost** — and at the center of it, **repair the democracy itself.** Before any outward ambition, the term's primary work is making the American house sound: its institutions, its social contract, its capacity to govern itself. This is the **keystone**, and like everything here it opens *diligence-first* — read the state of the thing before prescribing the fix.
2. **Mend with allies, alongside** — the nearest ring of relationships, repaired in step with the work at home (the [global posture](docs/global/00-the-posture.md)'s *amend, not abandon*).
3. **The wider contest — deferred.** The outward epic's [diligence](docs/global/diligence/) is mapped, but its **architecture is deliberately not yet drawn.** Planning the response to provocation before the home is sound is *reactive* doctrine; it waits until we know what we are doing and have a plan. We do not arm a posture we have not yet had to use.

The project's spine, pointed at sequence: **facts before fix — and self before world.**
